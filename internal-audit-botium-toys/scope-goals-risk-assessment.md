# Botium Toys – Scope, Goals, and Risk Assessment

## Audit Scope and Goals
**Scope:**  
The audit covers the entire security program at Botium Toys, including:

- Employee devices and equipment  
- Internal network and systems  
- Storefront and warehouse operations  
- Management of IT systems: accounting, telecommunications, databases, security, ecommerce, inventory

**Goals:**  
- Assess existing assets  
- Complete the controls and compliance checklist  
- Identify gaps and improve Botium Toys’ security posture

---

## Current Assets
- On-premises equipment for office use  
- Employee devices: desktops, laptops, smartphones, headsets, keyboards, docking stations  
- Storefront and warehouse products  
- IT systems and software: accounting, telecommunications, databases, security, ecommerce, inventory  
- Internet access and internal network  
- Data retention and storage  
- Legacy system maintenance

---

## Risk Assessment

**Risk Description:**  
- Inadequate asset management  
- Missing controls and compliance measures  
- Potential non-compliance with U.S. and international regulations

**Control Best Practices:**  
- Identify and classify assets (NIST CSF: Identify function)  
- Determine impact of asset loss on business continuity  
- Implement appropriate security controls

**Risk Score:** 8/10 – high, due to lack of controls and compliance adherence

### Additional Comments
- All employees can access sensitive data, including cardholder and PII/SPII  
- No encryption on locally stored credit card data  
- Access controls (least privilege, separation of duties) not enforced  
- Firewalls and antivirus are in place  
- No IDS implemented  
- No disaster recovery plans or backups  
- Breach notification plan for EU customers exists  
- Password policy weak and not enforced centrally  
- Legacy systems monitored without a schedule  
- Physical location secured with locks, CCTV, and fire prevention systems
